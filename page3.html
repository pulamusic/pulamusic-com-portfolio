music

Here is a copy of my doctoral dissertation: "Composing the African Atlantic: Sun Ra, Fela Anikulapo-Kuti, and the Poetics of African Diasporic Composition." In it I argue that we need to expand the definition of musical composition when considering African diasporic music to include the aspect of performance as an integral part of the composition. While this expanded definition covers more than just two musicians, I have chosen here to write about Fela and Sun Ra as exemplars of the kind of musical composition I am talking about: both composed intricate music and put on multi-dimensional, multi-media (for want of a better word) performances; both infused their music with a larger historical discourse, interacting with their audiences in ways that defy the commonly considered passive performer-audience dynamic; and so on.... Suffice to say, their music is great fun, and I had a blast writing this dissertation.
