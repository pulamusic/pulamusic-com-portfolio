American History 1 & 2

This list of PowerPoint slides includes the visual aids I created for teaching American History 1 & 2 at Springfield Technical Community College during the Spring 2017 semester. Please feel free to use them if you like, though with attribution.

My PowerPoint presentations follow the chapters of Eric Foner's two-volume work Give Me Liberty!: An American History - an excellent text for teaching American history.

Also of interest with regard to Foner's work is The YouTube series Crash Course U.S. History, which follows the narrative of Give Me Liberty! pretty closely. In fact, these videos are excellent supplements for teaching U.S. history with these texts.

The New World: The Columbian Revolution and its legacy in the Americas; Conquistadores.

Some Early English Definitions of Freedom: Some of the virtues and inconsistencies of early English settlement in North America.

A Timeline of the American Revolution: 1754-1783: An overview of the American Revolution, from early disagreements to outright conflict.

Republicanism & Liberalism: Politics of the early republic, feeding the creation of the United States.

Timeline for the United States Constitution: 1772-1808: From the Constitutional Convention to ratification to the Bill of Rights.

Securing the Republic: 1791-1815: The creation of the U.S. government, and events leading up to the War of 1812.

The Market Revolution: 1800-1860: A massive transformation of the American economy in the wake of the Revolution and ratification of the Constitution.

A House Divided: 1820-1861: Divisions between the North and South leading toward civil war.

Civil War: 1861-1865: North/South divisions boil over into Civil War

Reconstruction: An attempt to reconstruct the South in the wake of the Civil War.

The Gilded Age: 1870-1890: Industrial Revolution and the booming economy of late-19th century United States.

Freedom's Boundaries, At Home and Abroad: The limits of freedom during the Gilded Age.

The Progressive Era: 1900-1916: Progressive politics at the beginning of the 20th century.

World War I: The broader political events that led to the United States' involvement in the war.

The Twenties: Politics of the 1920s, leading up to the Great Depression.

The New Deal: The political reaction to the Depression.

World War II: The United States' involvement in WWII.

The United States and the Cold War: 1945-1953: Post WWII conflict between the U.S. and the Soviet Union.

An Affluent Society: The booming U.S. economy following WWII.

The Sixties: The liberalization of U.S. society in reaction to the conservatism of Cold War politics.

Affluenza: White Privilege and the Law: This is a presentation I created for my adult education students in response to a student's question about the word affluenza

A Timeline of U.S. Wars: This is a brief timeline of the major wars in U.S. history from the Revolutionary War to the current conflicts in Afghanistan, Syria, and Yemen. The idea behind this timeline is to help students develop a sense of the progression of conflict through U.S. history.

World War II and the Holocaust: This presentation presents a history of World War II with regard to the Holocaust.
